Hedy Lamarr

Actress and Inventor

We can say without a semblance of doubt that she was a woman who had an interesting life. She was curious, intelligent, and talented. She traveled and changed residence many times from a young age. She was the first to do many things on the big screen when films were still made in black and white while she was a star...

  • Was a film star from the 1930s to 1950s
  • Known as "The Most Beautiful Woman in Film"
  • Helped develop a radio guidance system for Allied torpedoes during WWII
  • Her work led to modern technologies such as Wi-Fi, CDMA, and Bluetooth
  • Inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame in 2014
